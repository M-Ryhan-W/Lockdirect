Auto locksmiths Manchester

Why a locksmith beats waiting for breakdown cover.

If you're locked out of your car and call your breakdown provider, you're often looking at a wait of 90 minutes to two hours, and they'll frequently send a recovery truck that can only force entry — leaving you with a damaged door card, broken window trim, or a snapped lock barrel.

Our auto-locksmiths carry professional non-destructive entry tools — air wedges, long-reach picks, electronic bypass equipment — and they're used to opening 5–10 cars a day. The vast majority of lockouts are resolved without a scratch on the paintwork.

Where we get called most

Car parks at Trafford Centre, Manchester Airport short-stay, M60 services at Stockport and Birch, Asda Hulme, ASDA Reddish, Tesco Stockport, Sainsbury's Heaton Park — anywhere people leave the keys in a coat or on a child seat and shut the door. Roadside callouts on the A6, A56, M60 hard shoulder, and supermarket car parks are our bread and butter.

The car-park scam to watch for

If you Google "car locksmith near me" you'll see search results dominated by national lead-generation sites that advertise £15 unlocks and then quote £400+ when they arrive. We list our prices upfront on this page, give a fixed quote on the phone, and never charge a call-out fee. If a locksmith quotes you a price that seems too good to be true at the roadside, walk away and call us.

FAQ

Car-lockout questions we get most.

Will you damage my car?
In nearly every callout, no. We use air wedges and long-reach tools that leave nothing behind. The only time damage is needed is if the lock or door mechanism has already failed mechanically — and we'd quote you for that before doing anything.
Can you open keyless / push-button start cars?
Yes. Most modern keyless cars still have a physical override channel in the door — we can usually open them mechanically even if the fob is dead or locked inside. For some makes we can also re-pair a replacement fob on-site.
What if my keys are locked in the boot?
Usually no problem — we open the cabin door first, then most cars have an internal boot release. On vans without an internal release, we have boot-specific bypass tools.
Can you cut a new key on the spot?
For most cars from 2005 onwards, yes — we cut and program a replacement key roadside. For cars older than that, sometimes we need to come back the next day with a specific blank, but you'll be driving on the first visit.
Do you charge more at night?
No premium for standard car lockouts at night — our 24/7 prices are flat. The only out-of-hours surcharge is on specialist commercial jobs.
What ID will the locksmith carry?
Photo ID, company branding on the van, and a fixed-price job sheet matching what you were quoted on the phone. Always ask to see it before they start — a real auto-locksmith will show it without being asked.
